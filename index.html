Why This Matters

Our group is focused on improving the immigration process because friends, family members, and entire communities are impacted by policies and practices that can feel unjust and harmful.

We are not seeking to abolish ICE. We are calling for reform that makes the process fair, efficient, and humane for immigrants and citizens alike.

Our Core Goals

  • Reduce detention uncertainty by improving communication timelines.
  • Ensure fair hearings and stronger due process access.
  • Increase transparency for families, lawyers, and communities.
  • Strengthen oversight when rights are violated or force is misused.
  • Support peaceful protest and policy advocacy at local and state levels.

The Problem We Are Addressing

Our proposal highlights concerns about wrongful detention, inconsistent policy enforcement, lack of accountability, and loopholes that can extend detention beyond expected timelines. These issues create fear and instability for families already navigating a difficult process.

We believe reform should include clear rules, stronger monitoring, reliable reporting, and accessible legal information so people can understand and defend their rights.

Case Study

Kilmar Abrego Garcia

In 2025, Kilmar Abrego Garcia was deported to El Salvador despite an existing immigration court protection that barred removal to that country. Court filings later described the deportation as an administrative error.

His case became a major due-process controversy. Federal courts, including the Supreme Court, ordered the government to facilitate his return. He was later returned to the U.S. and continued through legal proceedings.

Case Study

Lucia López Belloza

Lucia López Belloza, a 19-year-old Babson College freshman, was mistakenly deported to Honduras while traveling for Thanksgiving in November 2025. After federal courts ordered her return, she came back to the United States in early 2026.

Upon her return, Lucia successfully resumed her studies at Babson College and completed her dependent green card application. She graduated with her business degree and now works as a mentor helping other students navigate immigration challenges and their educational pursuits.
